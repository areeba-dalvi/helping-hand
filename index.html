. 02.Our SERVICES

EDUCATION SUPPORT :
Higher education is a critical link between learning and earning, which allows young people to thrive and transition to the pursuit of sustainable futures. Investments in higher education for refugees strengthen the national education systems in which they participate, to the benefit of host communities, students, and institutions. With higher education that is inclusive of refugees, all students can benefit from a richer academic environment, enhanced social cohesion, and improved academic infrastructure and resources.

HEALTHCARE AND NUTRITION:
The complete loss or deterioration of one’s eyesight can feel frightening, leaving those affected to wonder about their ability to maintain their independence, pay for medical care, retain employment, and provide for their families. HHRD conducts free eye exams and operations, provides proper medication and recovery workshops in Kenya, Mali, Nepal, Pakistan, Somalia, Tanzania, Uganda, Yemeni refugees in Somalia and South Africa.

SKILLS DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS:
HHRD currently provides training in 17 countries at Skills Development and Livelihood Centers set out to organize artisans, assist in developing products, improving product quality, and then marketing these products. Through talent assessment and skills advancement opportunities the beneficiaries are on the road to a better life with confidence to succeed. With an opportunity to work for a better life they also are given the confidence to succeed.

. 03.Our Projects

1-Gender-Based Violence
Gender-based violence (GBV) refers to harmful acts directed at an individual based on their gender. It is rooted in the abuse of power and includes acts that inflict physical and mental harm or suffering, threats of such acts, coercion or other acts of deprivation of liberty. Common forms of GBV towards the Rohingya refugee community include rape and commercial sexual exploitation.
2-Economic Empowerment
Income generating activities and trainings not only play a role in creating jobs and easing physical suffering but also help in healing mental wounds. Our Sewing & Stitching project enables women to learn new tailoring skills and gain independence while working from home. Our Handicraft project trains women with the machines and skills needed to create traditional handicrafts and start a small business to support themselves.

Renovation for Palestinian Refugees Homes
We have been implementing a Home Renovation project since 2019 for most deprived needy families living in 13 Refugee camps with special focus to GAZA and SOUF Camps. Many of the Palestinian Refugees as well as local poor Jordanians live in old homes which need maintenance. Some homes need construction of rooms, kitchens and bathrooms. As refugees and local families are unable to do this themselves, they keep on living in the same homes without sufficient space, repair and maintenance. HHRD objective is to make better living quality to those families by making necessary renovation and improvement in their homes, so they feel better and live in a healthier environment.

Necessities for a Brighter Future
With the cooperation of the Education Ministry, HHRD MENA (Middle East and North Africa) provides learning centers/classrooms along with restrooms, necessary furniture, and amenities, for refugee children and as relief for Jordan’s already overcrowded classrooms. Building these centers helps to facilitate out of school refugee children with access to primary elementary education that will help them pass the national tests required to enroll in formal schools.

Empower Youth Worldwide

HHRD’s Youth Empowerment Program is designed to mentor and cultivate today’s young leaders both in the US and abroad. Opportunities include collaborating with various youth organizations and universities to organize events; preparing containers of In-Kind Gifts and distributing items through our overseas “Mall of Humanity”; Career Counseling for students in the Education Support Program; Leadership Camps and Mentoring Sessions in Tanzania, Uganda, Kenya, and Pakistan; Youth Clubs in Pakistan; and Internship Programs in Jordan and Pakistan. Your support helps prepare today’s youth become tomorrow’s leaders

Youth Trips Abroad

HHRD trips allow youth "behind-the-scenes" access to field experiences showcasing humanitarian work. Through trips to Haiti, Pakistan, Kenya, and Jordan, youth ages 18-25 visit refugee camps and beneficiaries of HHRD’s flagship programs, as well as get hands on experience alongside trained professionals serving disadvantaged populations through healthcare, relief & development activities, and more. Trips average $3500/person where youth are responsible for 80% and HHRD covers the remaining 20%. Help empower youth and invest in the training of our future lead
